CODIS Cold Case Research

For this project, I conducted research on cold cases through the Federal Bureau of Investigation's website and wrote a detailed summary of a 1978 murder case. The primary goal was to investigate how large datasets, particularly DNA databases like CODIS, are used to support criminal investigations. This project also highlighted how modern technological advancements help solve crimes that have remained unsolved for decades.

The case I focused on involved a perpetrator whose crimes were only brought to trial thirty years after the events occurred.

Tools Used: World Wide Web, Microsoft Word

Through this experience, I gained a deeper appreciation for the crucial role that big data and data analysis software play in everyday investigative work and broader societal functions. It reinforced how the intersection of technology and law enforcement can bring justice even after many years.
